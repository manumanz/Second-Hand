how to play

you are the inside of a stranger's coat pocket, for seven days. you never see them — the swaying is them walking; the crush is them sitting; the stillness is night.

watch what falls in. every object is a clue about who they are — and one of them will turn out to matter more than the rest.

drag to nudge things. you are weak while they move and strong while they rest. wait for sitting, or night.

the hole (lower right seam) eats anything small that rests on it — and it grows every day. pull things to safety, or deliberately feed it something they'd be better off losing. rub across the hole to pull its threads tighter for a while.

the fingers dive in at story moments, hunting a specific object. leave it findable and the story goes one way. hide it in a corner — or down the hole — and the story goes another. both are allowed.

at week's end the coat is donated. everything you kept becomes someone else's beginning.
